The Space Race, a thrilling period of competition between the USA and USSR to conquer the cosmos, reached its peak between the late 1950s and the early 1970s. Buckle up, as we blast off on a journey through some of the most defining moments:

Gemini Missions

March 23, 1965: Gemini 3 astronauts Virgil Grissom and John Young perform the first spacewalk by Americans,and the first controlled spaceflight maneuvers.
December 15, 1965: Gemini 6A and Gemini 7 meet and perform the first rendezvous and docking of two spacecraft in orbit. (5)

Apollo 8

December 21, 1968: Apollo 8 carries astronauts Frank Borman, James Lovell, and William Anders on the first crewed mission to orbit the Moon, returning with breathtaking lunar photographs. (6)

Apollo 11

July 20, 1969: Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin become the first humans to walk on the Moon, uttering Armstrong's iconic words, "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." (7)
